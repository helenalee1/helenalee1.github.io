01

The context — product marketing at a tech startup

Context

Super.com is a consumer tech platform offering travel, financial products, and subscription services. The product marketing team operates at the intersection of data, messaging, and customer experience — making decisions quickly with limited margin for error across a large account base.

My work spanned two distinct areas: building testing infrastructure for smarter campaign decisions, and improving the AI chatbot that handled customer onboarding. Both required the same underlying skill — understanding where the customer experience was breaking down, and fixing it with evidence rather than assumption.

Why this context matters: Startup product marketing is fundamentally different from corporate or agency work. There is no playbook to inherit. Decisions move fast, ownership is broader, and the expectation is that you diagnose and solve, not wait to be told what to do.

Building the A/B testing framework

Testing infrastructure

I developed A/B testing frameworks and built a centralized learning hub to track results across experiments. Rather than running tests in isolation, the learning hub meant that findings from one campaign could inform the next — turning individual tests into a compounding knowledge base for the team.

Every test translated directly into targeting or messaging optimizations. All recommendations from the framework were adopted across the team.

03

Rebuilding the AI chatbot — quality and completion

Product work

Super.com's AI chatbot handled customer onboarding, but the experience had gaps. Customers were getting stuck at key moments, receiving unhelpful responses, or abandoning the journey before completing onboarding. I took on this problem independently, working with a partner to improve both the technical accuracy of the bot's responses and the human quality of its language.

Before and after — response quality
Super.com Onboarding Chat · Quality comparison
Before — bot response causing drop-off
Hi, I'm trying to complete my account setup but I'm not sure what documents I need to submit.
Please refer to our FAQ page for information about documentation requirements. Thank you for contacting us.
After — updated with FAQ content and natural language
Hi, I'm trying to complete my account setup but I'm not sure what documents I need to submit.
You'll need a government-issued photo ID and proof of address dated within the last 3 months. A utility bill or bank statement works great. Want me to walk you through the upload steps?
Pattern repeated across 100s of logged conversation examples — QA'd, revised, and re-tested to validate response quality
What the QA process involved
1
Logging and categorizing problem conversations
Reviewed historical chat logs to identify where customers were getting stuck, receiving irrelevant answers, or abandoning the onboarding flow. Built a structured log of failure patterns organized by customer intent and conversation stage.
2
Updating the knowledge source
Audited the official Super.com internal FAQ page to ensure the chatbot was pulling from accurate, current information. Updated response logs so answers reflected the latest product details and policies.
3
Creating test conversations to validate improvements
Wrote example conversations across the full range of customer intents — testing whether the revised bot could handle edge cases, guide customers through friction points, and keep the onboarding journey moving forward to completion.
The outcome: The revamped chatbot was validated as viable by the team, and the partner relationship continued based on the improvements made. Beyond the specific result, the project gave me a close-up view of where product experience and marketing intersect — how the quality of a customer's first interaction shapes every downstream conversion metric.

06

What I learned

Reflection
1
Testing is only as good as the system behind it
Running a single A/B test is straightforward. Building a learning hub that compounds across tests — where every result feeds the next decision — is a different challenge. The infrastructure matters as much as the individual experiment. That is what turns a test into a capability.
2
AI tools fail at the edges of human conversation
The chatbot QA work was a close study in where automated language breaks down. A bot can retrieve an answer. What it struggles with is reading the emotional register of a frustrated customer and adjusting tone accordingly. Improving that required thinking like a writer and a product manager at the same time.
3
Scale exposes the cost of inconsistency
Managing 15+ campaigns across 90+ accounts means that small inefficiencies multiply fast. UTM discipline, consistent briefing templates, and clean audience logic become critical when you are operating at that volume. Good process is what makes scale sustainable.
4
Amplitude data tells you where people go. Your job is figuring out why.
Behavioural data shows you the what — which segments convert, which drop off, which features get used. The strategic work is building a coherent explanation for the pattern, then designing a response to it. Data gives you the question. You still have to find the answer.
