The challenge

Context

When I joined Microsoft Canada's Modern Work & Security team, the Cloud Skills Challenge was an online certification platform helping professionals become Microsoft-certified that had launched with early success but needed a stronger growth engine behind it.

The learning content was strong, but the program was not deeply embedded in the sales conversations happening every day across Microsoft's account teams. Entire audience segments, including a largely untapped education sector, had not been meaningfully reached.

My role: I was given end-to-end ownership across go-to-market strategy, content development, sales enablement, and day-to-day operations — with one goal: get more people in and keep them engaged.

Starting with the people, not the product

Audience research

Before building anything, I spent time talking directly with SMC and enterprise account executives to understand how they were currently talking about skilling with their clients, what friction they ran into, and what they actually needed to make the Cloud Skills Challenge part of their conversations naturally.

I studied historic strategies to understand what had worked before and where there was room to do something different. From this, I built a persona framework that mapped distinct audience segments to the certification pathways most relevant to them.

Key insight: When participant data pointed to unmet demand for a Teams Administrator learning track, I worked with the PM team to get it added to the platform, turning an audience signal into a product decision.

What I built and owned

My contribution
A
Sales enablement suite
I built a full suite of enablement materials such as presentations, one-pagers, email templates, social media copy direction and other digital assets, and secured time in all-hands meetings to put the program on senior AEs' radars. I made sure to listen and understand the majority of the pain points of the AE's and found ways to ease these gaps by creating and enhancing Cloud Skills Challenge assets for teams to use in their day-to-day client conversations nautrally. The SMC team became some of the program's strongest internal champions from that point forward.
B
GTM strategy and persona framework
I mapped distinct customer personas across key segments: Azure users, security professionals, modern workplace users, and non-technical audiences. I connected each to the certification pathways most relevant to their role. Identified the education sector as a new, overlooked growth channel and developed an initiative for AEs to introduce the program in client conversations.
C
Event activation — Colloque Cybersecurity Event
Served as creative lead for the Cloud Skills Challenge's presence at Colloque Cybersecurity, developing all bilingual (EN/FR) materials from scratch and supporting the account executive on the ground. The activation drove a record number of sign-ups on the Information Protection Administrator pathway in a single event because the right materials, the right audience, and the right timing came together.
D
Demand generation strategy presentation
Closed the term by presenting the full demand generation strategy to 40+ account executives, where the approach was well received by GTM leadership.

What I learned

Reflection
1
Sales and marketing are the same conversation
The biggest unlock was not a campaign. It was getting into the rooms where AEs were already talking to clients and making the program easy for them to introduce. Marketing that enables and suppports sales to carry the message travels further than any campaign built independently.
2
Start with friction, not features
Talking directly to account executives before building anything revealed that the program's gap was not awareness, it was embeddedness in sales conversations. That insight shaped everything else. I learned to diagnose before prescribing.
3
Audience data is a product brief
When participant data showed demand for a Teams Administrator track that did not exist yet, I worked with the PM team to get it built. Marketing insights do not have to stop at campaigns, they can shape the product itself.
