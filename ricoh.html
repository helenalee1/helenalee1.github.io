The problem

Context

Ricoh Canada had already invested in GaggleAMP — an employee advocacy platform designed to help sales teams extend the company's reach on LinkedIn through their personal networks. The problem was that nobody was really using it. There was no content system, no onboarding process, and no clear way for account executives or product marketing managers to know what to share or when. The platform existed, the licence was paid for, and it was completely dormant.

Understanding the audience

Insight

This project had two distinct internal audiences with completely different needs — and getting the platform right meant understanding both of them separately.

Account executives needed content that was immediately relevant to their specific clients and sectors. If a post had nothing to do with their book of business, they would scroll past it and never come back. The platform had to make relevance effortless.

Product marketing managers needed a structured way to funnel approved, on-brand content into the platform without creating extra work. They were the supply side — and if the workflow was too complex, content would dry up fast regardless of how good the architecture was.

The core insight: Marketing serves sales best when it anticipates their context. AEs don't have time to find content. Relevance delivered at the right moment is what turns a passive tool into a daily habit.

05

What I learned

Reflection
1
Adoption is a design problem, not a communication problem
The platform had been available for months before I arrived. Sending a Slack message telling people to use it would not have fixed anything. What fixed it was removing the reasons not to — unclear feeds, no onboarding, irrelevant content. Frictionless beats compelling every time.
2
Marketing serves sales best when it anticipates their context
The department-specific feed architecture was the most important structural decision I made. Relevance delivered at the right moment is what turns a passive tool into a daily habit. That principle applies well beyond advocacy platforms.
3
Building from zero teaches you what infrastructure actually means
Having full ownership of a 0-to-1 build — no existing system to inherit, no playbook to follow — taught me how to sequence decisions. You cannot train people on a platform that is not organized. You cannot fill an organized platform without a content workflow. Order matters.
